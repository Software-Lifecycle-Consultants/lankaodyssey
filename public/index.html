Event DNA

Built for the deeply committed.

Lanka Odyssey is not a race you enter on a whim. It is a self-navigated endurance challenge across one of the world's most culturally and topographically intense island interiors.

Self-Navigated

The checkpoints are fixed. The path between them is yours. Plan, adapt, and push through using your own judgement — there is no route sheet handed to you.

Daily Cut-off Clock

Every day has a compulsory checkpoint and a fixed cut-off time. Arrive late and your official ride is over. Pace, fuel, and sleep decisions carry real consequences.

Fully Unsupported

No team cars. No drop bags. No private support crew. Riders source food, water, lodging, and mechanical help from whatever resources the road provides.

Live GPS Dot-Watch

Every rider wears an approved live tracker from the start gun to the finish line. Your position streams in real time — family, friends, and spectators can follow every move.

Why Ride Lanka Odyssey

The island fights back.
That is the point.

Sri Lanka is small on a map and enormous on a bicycle. In seven days you will encounter almost every terrain type the tropics can produce.

Coastal Flats & Headwinds

The west and north coasts are ruler-flat, which sounds easy — until the Indian Ocean trades hit at 35 km/h. Days 1 and 2 are a war of patience against the wind before the island turns up its gradient.

Dry Zone Heat

The north-central dry zone regularly hits 38 °C in August. The roads shimmer. Water stops are sparse. Days 3 and 4 demand ruthless hydration discipline and a sun-smart start time — both built into the cut-off schedule.

Jungle & Wildlife Roads

The northeast corridor and the hill-country descents thread through national park buffer zones. Elephants, leopards, sloth bears, and peacocks share the road. Ride dawn and dusk with awareness — this is their territory first.

Hill Country Climbs

Day 6 is the defining stage. From Arugam Bay at sea level, riders climb over 3,000 m of cumulative elevation through Kandy's switchbacks, Nuwara Eliya's cloud forests, and the Ella Gap at 1,041 m. There is no detour around it.

Ancient Kingdom Roads

Between the ruins of Anuradhapura, Yapahuwa, Kurunegala, Sigiriya, and Polonnaruwa, the roads are provincial B-roads with rough asphalt and monsoon cracking. Gravel tyres are not mandatory — but they are wise.

Ocean Finish

The final 200 km trace the southern Indian Ocean coastline. Coral-white beaches, Dutch colonial fort walls, and a sea breeze that smells of salt and relief. Galle Fort's stone arch is one of the most iconic finish lines in endurance cycling.

FAQ

Questions answered.

Is this a race?

Lanka Odyssey is an unsupported endurance odyssey, not a traditional mass-start race. All riders share the same start window and daily cut-off clocks. Riders are ranked by completion status and overall elapsed time, but the primary goal is legal, self-supported completion within the time limit.

Can I choose my own route?

Yes — between mandatory checkpoints the road is entirely yours. You decide how to connect the dots using any publicly accessible road. The checkpoint locations are fixed and non-negotiable. Everything else is your navigation decision.

Is GPS tracking mandatory?

Yes. Every official rider must carry an approved GPS tracker from the moment they leave the start line until they cross the finish or officially retire. Extended signal loss or disabling the device will trigger an official inquiry and may result in disqualification.

What is the entry process?

Submit your application with rider details. The team reviews all submissions and contacts accepted riders with a secure payment link. Your place is not confirmed until payment is complete. The field is capped at 40 riders.

What fitness level is required?

Riders should have demonstrated back-to-back ultra-endurance performance on a bicycle. Experience with bikepacking, randonneuring, or ultra-cycling events is expected. The cut-offs are fixed and unforgiving — first-time long-distance riders are not recommended to apply.

What bikes are allowed?

Any human-powered bicycle is permitted. Road, gravel, and mountain bikes have all successfully completed similar events. The terrain varies significantly across the island — gravel tyre clearance and robust luggage solutions are strongly recommended.

Why Sri Lanka?

Sri Lanka is one of the most geographically and culturally dense islands on earth. In a country roughly the size of Ireland, riders encounter tropical coastline, desert-dry scrubland, 2,000-year-old UNESCO capitals, cloud-forest tea estates at nearly 2,000 m, and one of Asia's most celebrated surf beaches — all connected by a road network that ranges from fast A-roads to crumbling jungle tracks. No other island offers this concentration of challenge and beauty at ultra-cycling scale.

The August window is deliberately chosen: the southwest monsoon has retreated, the northeast coast is dry, and temperatures in the north and east are at their most manageable. It is the single best month to ride the complete island circuit.

What is the significance of the UNESCO World Heritage Sites on the route?

The route passes five UNESCO World Heritage Sites, each representing a distinct chapter of Sri Lankan civilisation:

  • Anuradhapura (Day 3 cut-off) — Sri Lanka's first and longest-reigning ancient capital, inhabited from the 4th century BC. The dagoba stupas here are among the oldest surviving structures on earth and sacred pilgrimage sites for Theravada Buddhists worldwide.
  • Sigiriya (Day 4) — A 5th-century rock fortress and palace complex rising 200 m from the jungle floor. The frescoes, mirror-wall inscriptions, and water gardens rank among the greatest urban planning achievements of the ancient world.
  • Dambulla Cave Temple (Day 4) — A complex of five cave sanctuaries with over 150 Buddha statues and 2,100 m² of murals, continuously maintained for 22 centuries.
  • Polonnaruwa (Day 4 cut-off) — The second ancient capital, built in the 11th–13th centuries. The Gal Vihara rock carvings and the Parakrama Samudra irrigation tank reflect an empire at the height of its engineering capability.
  • Kandy — Sacred City of the Tooth Relic (Day 6) — The last royal capital of Sri Lanka, home to the Temple of the Tooth which holds what is revered as the Buddha's tooth relic. Kandy's annual Esala Perahera procession is one of Asia's great cultural spectacles.

Important Notice

Health, Safety & Risk

Health & Medical Fitness

All participants must be in excellent physical health and hold a current medical clearance from a licensed physician confirming fitness for ultra-endurance cycling. The event organisers reserve the right to require medical documentation before accepting any application. Riders with pre-existing cardiac, respiratory, or musculoskeletal conditions are strongly advised to seek specialist advice before applying. The event is not suitable for individuals without prior ultra-distance cycling experience.

Personal Health Insurance

Participation in Lanka Odyssey requires valid personal health and travel insurance that explicitly covers ultra-endurance sporting events, emergency medical evacuation, and repatriation. Riders are solely responsible for arranging and maintaining adequate cover throughout the event period, including pre- and post-event travel. The event organisers do not provide medical insurance of any kind. Proof of insurance will be required as part of the rider acceptance process.

Wildlife & Environmental Hazards

The Lanka Odyssey route passes through or adjacent to national parks, forest reserves, and wildlife corridors. Sri Lanka's endemic wildlife — including Asian elephants, leopards, sloth bears, crocodiles, and venomous snakes — may be encountered on the road, particularly at dawn and dusk. Riders must not approach, feed, or antagonise wildlife under any circumstances. All riders should carry a first-aid kit and be aware of relevant emergency contact procedures for wildlife encounters.

Road & Traffic Safety

Sri Lankan roads present real safety challenges including mixed-traffic highways, unsealed surfaces, livestock crossings, and limited road-lighting in rural areas. All riders must comply with local traffic laws at all times and wear approved helmets throughout the event. Night riding is permitted but requires functioning front and rear lights as a condition of classification. The event organisers are not liable for any road traffic incidents during the event.

Liability Waiver

By submitting an application and completing registration, all participants agree to the Lanka Odyssey full liability waiver and event terms and conditions, which will be provided to accepted riders. Participation is entirely at the rider's own risk. Lanka Odyssey, its organisers, volunteers, and sponsors accept no liability for injury, illness, loss, damage, or death arising from participation in the event.
